Ilona – Conversation, Part 3, Reporting

[The agenda, a retro, necessary notes, no progress without evaluation]

InterTaG, the Internet of Transformation and Governance – what an idea! "It has to be so simple that everyone understands it," Rosho had said during a short video call after our last messaging exchange. "It has to be understandable at the level of a 12-year-old. We'll probably design InterTaG as virtual reality, I'll appear in it as an avatar and help people get to grips with the self-learning platform and the implementation of iterations."
Are we perhaps crazy after all? First InterTaG, then virtual reality and avatars... It sounds so crazy that it must be good, because otherwise it's totally gaga. Again and again, I ask myself what kind of parallel universe I have wonderfully strayed into with Rosho.World. And I'm talking to a virtual 13-year-old right now.

Ilona: Okay, the calendar is set up, the appointment is confirmed. What next?
Me: You prepare for the meeting by writing an agenda and sending it out. Item one on the agenda—presentation of the project: "Transformation of bookbinders with evolutionary governance, because conventional strategies have not yielded satisfactory results."
Ilona: Oh, no! Not only will Grandma immediately drop out, but so will everyone else.
Me: Then keep it low-key by saying, hey guys, I think I've noticed some friction and friction in the recent past and would like to help make things run more smoothly. What do you think?
Ilona: Great, everyone will say.
Me: Then on to item two on the agenda. The rules for communication during meetings would be a good place to start. But I would rather begin with a review. How have things gone over the last few years? Good? Not so good? Really bad?
Ilona: Yes, first the retro, then the rules. That way, the beginning won't be so dry.
Me: As a facilitator, it's important that you stay calm, keep track of everything, and, above all, take notes.
Ilona: I can stay calm and keep track of things. I'll have to force myself to take notes.
Me: Unfortunately, notes are necessary. You need an inventory that can be used as a basis for the current situation. There is another aspect to this. Once everyone, including you, has said what went how, it would be helpful if each of you rated each individual event on a scale of 0 to 100 percent. Zero percent is really miserable, total crap, i.e., as bad as it gets. 100 is top, i.e., the optimum.
Ilona: Why do we need that?
Me: So that we can derive progress from it later. Positive as well as negative, called reporting.
Ilona: What if someone wants to give 120 percent?
Me: It sounds tempting, but it's outside the scale, so it's an invalid value and not usable.
Ilona: What a bummer.

Learned in Chapter 26:
A good start: a retro. What has gone well in recent years? What has been bad? Where is there a current need for action? Every retro needs a facilitator to organize and optimize the process. A retro serves to take stock. All aspects that can be evaluated should also be evaluated – on a scale from 0 to 100%. This enables the mathematization of history (based on further retros) and a " " reporting, i.e., the calculation and derivation of progress. This applies to all projects. Small, large, and very large.
