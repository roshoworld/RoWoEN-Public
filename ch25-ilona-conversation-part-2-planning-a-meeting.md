# 25. Ilona -- Conversation, Part 2, Planning a Meeting

Ilona – Conversation, Part 2 , planning a meeting

[The InterTaG, the facilitator, rules, and one variable]

As I write this, a window pops up on my computer in my home office. A message from Rosho. "After decades of searching for solutions," he writes, "I have come to realize that the method is the solution. There is no solution to all the problems in the world. But there is a method for addressing the problems. And that is precisely the solution. Do you understand?"
"I think so," I reply.
"It's the solution because iteration, combined with self-organization, implies constant improvement. The method for solving problems is thus practically built in."
Excited, I hammer the term "self-optimization" into the keys.
"This is going to go viral," he writes back.
Let's assume that the approximately 86 billion neurons in my brain are currently processing a thought. Perhaps it's like a huge spider's web, except that the subtle vibrations are triggered by mental activity and thus initiate more or less meaningful actions. All of this happens billions of times at the same moment. Namely, in all those brains that are currently awake or dreaming. A significant proportion of these brains are also sitting at a computer at this very moment, triggering socially networked impulses that in turn cause vibrations in networks. And then there is the emergence of artificial intelligence that observes these activities and learns from them. What this feedback from networked AI could mean for the future is becoming increasingly clear.
"I just secured the domain intertag.org," Rosho reports and explains to me: "Everyone knows the Internet of Information. The Internet of Things, too, by now. The Internet of Assets, e.g., non-fungible tokens, is just becoming known. Most people have heard little to nothing about the Internet of Energy so far, but that is also emerging. And we are now building the Internet of Transformation and Governance. In short: InterTaG."
For a moment, I stare at the abbreviation without thinking anything. My computer also seems to be holding its breath. Nothing is moving on the screen. Maybe Rosho got a call from a customer or the connection was lost for some other reason. But suddenly, another line from him: "What do you think?"
How should I answer such a question? Super great? It's more than that. Rosho has a broad vision. You could call it a global perspective. But even that doesn't seem sufficient to describe it, because he also puts what he sees into practice. And I find this practical impulse really impressive.
"Great," I reply.
"What are you doing right now?"
"Talking to Ilona, the bookbinder's daughter."
His answer: a smiley face.

Sorry, Ilona, kept you waiting. As I said, Rosho.World provides the map, or rather the GPS, i.e., clues to help you get ahead, because maps ... 
Ilona: ...are so yesterday.
Me: But you and your family have to set off yourselves.
Ilona: What's the next step? 
Me: A meeting with as many people involved as possible, preferably everyone. Then you present the project.
Ilona: Great! I'm really looking forward to it. I'm good at talking. I'm class president for the third time.
Me: Very good, then you're suited to be the facilitator. That's the person who schedules meetings and makes sure that these meetings run according to certain agreements.
Ilona: How do I prevent Mom and Dad from starting to argue in the middle of it?
Me: By setting guidelines and rules for meetings.
Ilona: Which we'll come up with here and now? I have some ideas...
Me: Great! Setting up the meeting rules would be an important point for the first meeting. You can contribute your ideas there. But of course, the meeting participants should be informed in advance that this point is planned for the first meeting. The best way to do this is to set up a shared calendar that everyone has access to. Ideally, it should be cloud-based so that you can invite people with just a few clicks and don't have to send emails or possibly handwritten letters. Then you'll automatically receive confirmations and won't have to follow up.
Ilona: Sold. I already know how to do that.

Learned in Chapter 25:
The method is the solution. To provide this method for solutions, the InterTaG platform is to be built. InterTaG stands for "Internet of Transformation and Governance." And: If you want to drive transformation, you need meetings with the people involved. These meetings should be conducted according to rules agreed upon by the participants.
